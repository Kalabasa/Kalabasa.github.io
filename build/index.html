About Me

I started programming when I was just young boy. Even before that, I have already been making things with pen and paper, LEGO bricks, stuff, and video game level editors. I currently inhabit the Philippines. I like games, programming, art, and music, or any combination of those. As it happens, the most exciting combination of those four things is video game development, and that is why I want to work more on that field.

Other stuff that interest me are design, music theory, cinematography, and engineering. Although I'm more interested in the processes than the products themselves

Computer science topics that look interesting to me include, but are not limited to: algorithms and data structures, artifical intelligence, computer graphics, and human-computer interaction (or UX). Incidentally, gamedev encompasses everything in this list.

On games: I enjoy playing puzzle games and innovative games (Portal is a great example of both). I rarely play video games these days. More than games, I also like game design, which is a neat field, or art if you want to consider it as an art. It is essentially the engineering of fun.

Music that I listen to currently are smooth jazz and chill hip-hop music, but I don't really like rapping or vocals. I believe the combination is called jazzhop. Sometimes, I also make music.

On visual arts... Well, I just like looking at nice pictures and drawings. I can't create them though, in a way that makes me feel satisfied about it. I'm not really into some of the advanced art you can find in museums. Sadly, they communicate nothing to me. I just like the colors of the sunset, the lone tree in the winter, the play of light and colors in a fantasy painting, the bloom filter on a seamless skybox, the fluidity of a well-done pixel art animation, etc.

I have attempted, with varying degrees of success, to mix and combine these interests into single projects, like a random music generator, procedural art, video game music, game art, game AI, and so on.
